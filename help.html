About the Survey Explorer

The survey explorer is a useful tool to help you find which survey may have the information you need. You can use the search bar or the dropdown lists to select geographies, frequencies, topics, or subtopics you are interested in.

All program and survey listings will take you to their respective website where you can find more information about the program/survey. For example, contact info, data, news releases, and more.

Frequently Asked Questions
Why does the search bar not produce the survey results I would expect?

The search bar works best when using specific keywords only found in in this tool. It is not able to function as a traditional search engine. It is sensitive to spelling. Please use the all the guidance pages for suggested search terminology.

Why can I not find my specific state (ex. Maryland)?

Please use the dropdown filter under geographies “State”, navigate to your survey of interest, and then access data from there or using our data tools.

Can I select multiple boxes at a time?

Yes! You can select multiple selections within a dropdown list as well as across multiple dropdowns. For example, you can select National and State geographies as well as Economic and Business topics.

How do I know what topic terms to use when I search?

We provided a list of subtopics for you to look for the right search terms in the tool while using the search bar.

What do I do if I don’t see the geography I need in the dropdown?

There are many geographies, if you cannot find your geography, select “Other” and try to use other filters, such as topic and subtopics, to narrow your search. See the geography guidance page to see what was included under “Other”.

Why can't I find data for all the years of a survey?

Some surveys have been changed or combined into other program areas over time and therefore, only the most recent data is available.
